# Scarborough Shoal Standoff Public Record

## Approved Course Claim

The Scarborough Shoal standoff shows the West Philippine Sea dispute as observable state behavior, not only as a legal abstraction. Official vessels, contested access, maritime presence, and competing claims turned the shoal into a concrete example of how states act around disputed waters before and after formal legal processes.

## Course Use

Use this source for suggested prompts about state behavior around the dispute, especially when explaining why institutions and law operate alongside diplomacy, capacity, and facts on the water. The teaching point is that maritime disputes are shaped by what states do in practice as well as by the rules they invoke.
