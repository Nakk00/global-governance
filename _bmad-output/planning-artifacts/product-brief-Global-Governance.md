# Product Brief: Global Governance

## Executive Summary

Global Governance is a premium single-page educational web experience for GNED-07: The Contemporary World. It turns a dense academic topic into an interactive documentary-style journey that helps students understand, explain, and present global governance with confidence.

The product combines guided scrollytelling, an interactive United Nations module, a West Philippine Sea case study, and a grounded chatbot that answers from approved project materials while clearly showing when support is weak or out of scope. The goal is not just to inform. It is to help users build a clear mental model of how global governance works, where it succeeds, and where it breaks down in practice.

## The Problem

Global governance is often taught as a set of abstract definitions, institutions, and legal ideas that students can memorize but struggle to connect. Traditional reports, slide decks, and static websites usually present the topic in a way that is correct but hard to retain, hard to explain, and weak as a live presentation asset.

This project addresses that gap by making the topic visible, structured, and concrete. It gives students a better way to learn the material, a better way to present it, and a better way to answer follow-up questions during review or demo time.

## The Product Idea

The experience is designed as a guided narrative with strong visual identity and clear educational pacing. Users move through a sequence that introduces the idea of global governance, explains the major actors involved, shows the role and limits of the United Nations, and then grounds the lesson in the West Philippine Sea case study.

The core experience includes:

- a strong hero section that establishes tone and credibility
- scrollytelling sections that keep the learning flow coherent
- the UN Command Center for exploring institutional roles and limits
- the West Philippine Sea Interactive Dossier for applied understanding
- a grounded chatbot for clarification, recap, and source-aware support
- visible references so the academic basis of the content stays transparent

The product should feel like an interactive academic documentary, not a generic school website.

## Why It Stands Out

The differentiator is the combination of premium presentation and academic discipline. The experience is visually memorable, but every major interaction is meant to improve comprehension rather than distract from it.

Design and motion choices reinforce that goal. Motion and Lenis keep the experience fluid and easy to follow, while GSAP is reserved only for rare showcase scenes that truly need it. That balance keeps the product polished without letting motion dominate the learning experience.

The chatbot is also a major differentiator. It is not positioned as an open-ended assistant. It is a bounded academic support tool that helps users trust the experience because it stays within approved materials and clearly signals when support is weak or out of scope.

## Primary Users

- Student presenters who need to understand the topic well enough to explain it clearly in class
- Classmates who want a faster and more engaging way to learn the material
- Professors or evaluators who want evidence of academic rigor, source discipline, and polished execution

## What Success Looks Like

The product is successful when users leave the site with a clearer and more confident understanding of global governance than they would get from a static report or slide deck.

Success also means:

- users can explain the topic in their own words after the walkthrough
- the learning flow feels complete and easy to follow
- the UN module and case study make abstract ideas concrete
- the chatbot stays grounded, on-topic, and trustworthy
- the site works reliably in a live presentation setting
- the interface remains readable, responsive, and accessible on common student hardware

## Scope

### MVP

The MVP should include:

- the complete learning flow from introduction to conclusion
- the hero experience
- the UN Command Center
- the West Philippine Sea Interactive Dossier
- the grounded chatbot
- public-chat protection with rate limiting, abuse counters, and short cooldowns
- clear references and source visibility
- responsive and accessibility-conscious presentation across desktop and mobile, without learner account setup

### Post-MVP

Future expansion can add:

- the full Living Globe Hero
- Student / Expert mode
- richer chatbot UX and guidance
- additional motion polish
- the Global Governance Simulator as a future stretch feature

### Out of Scope

The current brief does not call for:

- a general-purpose open-domain assistant
- LMS integration or student accounts
- a full CMS or publishing platform
- heavy 3D scenes beyond selectively approved showcase moments

## Product Principles

- Clarity before spectacle
- Trust before novelty
- Guided exploration over fragmented navigation
- Premium presentation with graceful fallback
- Academic rigor that is visible, not implied

## Closing Takeaway

Global Governance is more than a school project site. It is a presentation-ready learning product built to make a difficult topic easier to understand, easier to remember, and easier to defend in a classroom setting.

If it succeeds, users should finish the experience feeling informed, oriented, and ready to explain the topic themselves.
