Step 9 Visual Exploration

Six design directions for a premium academic experience.

These directions apply the approved UX foundation to the Global-Governance site. Each one explores a different balance of cinematic storytelling, academic clarity, interaction weight, and presentation confidence. Direction 1 is the recommended base, but the strongest final answer may be a hybrid.

Primary Goal Help student presenters understand, navigate, and explain the topic with confidence.
Shared Foundation Deep navy, parchment reading surfaces, muted gold, restrained teal, editorial typography, and layered explanation.
Flagship Moments Hero arrival, UN Command Center, West Philippine Sea dossier, and source-aware chatbot states.
Recommendation Start from Diplomatic Editorial, then borrow structure from Atlas and evidence styling from Dossier.
Direction Set

Visual paths worth comparing

Use these sections to judge hierarchy, tone, motion weight, and how the content feels in a presentation context. The aim is not to pick a perfect mockup untouched, but to identify the strongest combination for the final site.

Comparison Matrix

Recommended hybrid

The best final system is not a single unmodified direction. It is a composed solution: Direction 1 for the whole-site baseline, Direction 2 for structure, Direction 5 for the case-study sub-language, and Direction 6 for the UN flagship module.

Direction Strongest Use Primary Benefit Main Risk Keep / Borrow
1. Diplomatic Editorial Full-site baseline Best balance of atmosphere, clarity, and academic trust Can feel too safe if the hero and modules are not pushed enough Keep as the overall base
2. Strategic Atlas Navigation and systems sections Excellent structure and mental-model support Less emotional lift than the top directions Borrow hierarchy and map logic
3. Documentary Cinema Hero and chapter transitions Highest impact and strongest emotional beat Easy to overdo Borrow selected transition language only
4. Institutional Ledger References and dense reading surfaces Highest scholarly trust and long-form readability Not dramatic enough for the whole product Borrow paper surfaces and citation treatment
5. Casefile Immersion West Philippine Sea module Best at timeline tension and evidence framing Too specialized for the full site Keep as the case-study sub-language
6. Command Narrative UN Command Center Best interactive system feel Can drift into dashboard energy Keep as the UN module accent language

Recommended lock-in: use Direction 1 as the product-wide foundation, then layer Direction 2 for information architecture, Direction 5 for the case-study experience, and Direction 6 for the UN interaction model. This gives the site a coherent main language with sharper, memorable sub-languages where they matter most.
